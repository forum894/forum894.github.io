`Origin and development Godzilla is an old character that has gone through many periods, so there are many variations in shape, size and origin. In the early 1950s, TOHO released the Godzilla series, which is considered a prehistoric creature that "wakes up" from nuclear tests.

Japan's Godzilla is categorized into three different eras: Showa (Showa, Emperor Hirohito's reign), Heisei, and Millennium series (1999 – 2004). In the Heisei period series (Peace, during the reign of Emperor Akihito), Godzilla is a type of prehistoric dinosaur known as Godzillasaurus, which survived in the 1940s in the Marshall Islands. Time travelers from the future brought Godzillasaurus to the Bering Sea, only to expose it to radiation from a Russian submarine and become Godzilla, in the 1970s.

In GMK (Godzilla, Mothra and King Ghidorah: Giant Monsters All-Out Attack), Godzilla was originally a monster that was killed in 1954 by the Oxygen Destroyer created by Dr. Daisuke Serizawa. However, Godzilla's body did not decompose, but was raised from the dead by the spirits of the dead during World War II, to punish Japan for its sins.

During the Showa period (Showa, Emperor Hirohito's reign) and the Millennium series (1999 - 2004), Godzilla was a prehistoric reptile living in the middle of the land and sea; retreated to the depths of the sea after the Cretaceous Great Cretaceous and was awakened by the American hydrogen bomb test.

For the most part, Godzilla of the Showa period was considered a hero, but in the Heisei period it was mostly considered a villain. By Toho's 2016 Shin Godzilla, it's still seen as a villain. However, in MonsterVerse, Godzilla is depicted as a Savior of nature, purging Kaiju (giant beasts) that threaten the existence of this beautiful planet, as we have seen in the series. 2014 American film.
