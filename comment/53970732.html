bài này mình viết tiếng anh nhé 1.Eel

The eel has a long body like that of a snake, and a mouth with a wide jaw. This fish can be up to 2.5m long. They usually hide in crevices or rock holes during the day and often hunt at night. With sharp teeth and strong jaws , the eel's bites cause major injuries . In addition, these large wounds are also very susceptible to infection by bacteria residing in the mouth of this fish.

2. Lampreys Previously, lampreys only appeared in the Atlantic Ocean. However, in the 19th century, this fish entered the Great Lakes, USA after people dug the Erie Canal. With extremely high adaptability, this fish has survived and developed very quickly in freshwater environments and has become the culprit causing the decline of many other fish species in this lake area. Lampreys live as parasites on other animals by sucking the blood of these animals. Lampreys have a round mouth with lots of teeth growing around the mouth. They use their mouths to cling to the skin of other fishes, passing their sharp, pointed tongues through the scales to suck blood. The prey often die after being lamprey blood . 3. Vampire Fish The vampire fish is also known as the Vandellia cirrhosa catfish . This is a freshwater fish found only in the Amazon region. Small in size and bright white, vampire fish are difficult to spot in the wild. However, this fish is extremely sensitive to ammonia in the water, which is normally released through the gills of other fish. They often follow the trail of ammonia to seek refuge and feed on the blood in the gills of larger fish. This fish can also enter the human body through secretions and live in it. In 1997, someone in the Amaron area had a vampire fish enter their body after bathing in the river. 4.Fighting fish The fighting fish is the most venomous animal in the water. Fighting fish do not attack humans, but with their superior stealth art, fighting fish often confuse other animals and step on them. At that time, the thorns containing the venom will exert their power. The venom of the fighting fish can cause shock, instant paralysis and death if not treated promptly. 5.Snakehead fish Inhabiting most of Southeast Asia, parts of India and Africa, the snakehead fish with its sharp teeth is a real monster. The usual length of this fish is 0.6m and can be up to 0.9m. In addition to being poisonous, the snakehead will eat everything in its path . It can also survive on land for up to 4 days. Therefore, this fish is a terror to other animals . 6. Piranha fish Piranhas are carnivorous fish . They eat both terrestrial and aquatic animals. Piranhas feed throughout the day and can rip off their prey within minutes. A school of piranhas can " eaten " a cow in 10 minutes . Scientists say about 1,200 cows are killed and eaten by this fish each year in Brazil. Children playing in the river and women washing clothes near the shore can also be victims of this fish. Piranha fish has a triangular body, razor sharp teeth, large teeth in the lower jaw and small teeth in the upper jaw. A special feature is that Piranha's teeth are replaceable. When a tooth is broken, a new tooth will grow in its place. The teeth of this fish are so sharp that it is used by the Amaron people as hair scissors . [color=#FF0024]
