  11. Neutron stars can rotate at 600 rpm When a star enters the neutron star stage, it has reached one of the last points of the planet's evolution. These massive stars were born in supernova explosions, but they collapsed to their cores by a centripetal gravity, then spun extremely rapidly as a physical consequence. of that process. Normally, neutron stars can rotate up to 60 revolutions per second after being born. But in a special case, this speed can increase to more than 600 rpm.

But since there is no air in the vacuum of the universe, there is always an eerie silence out there. In contrast, on Earth there is an atmosphere and air pressure that allows sound to travel. That explains why there is so much noise underground.

  13. The number of stars in the universe is an uncountable number Basically, we don't know exactly how many stars there are in the universe. But scientists can use estimates to answer the question: How many stars are there in our galaxy, the Milky Way? They then multiplied that number by their best guess of the number of galaxies in the universe. After all that math, NASA can only confidently say that the number of stars in this universe is immeasurable, too many to be counted. A study by the Australian National University estimated that there are 70,000 million million million stars in the universe.

  14. The footprints that the Apollo astronauts left on the Moon will probably last for at least another 100 million years. The Moon has no atmosphere, wind or water to wash away or erase the imprints of the Apollo astronauts. That means the footprints, rover wheel tracks, traces left by human spacecraft will be kept on the moon for a very long time. The only thing that can erase these traces is the deposition of cosmic dust that is sucked up to the surface of the Moon. These are "micrometer meteorites" that are constantly hitting the Moon, but this process will happen very, very slowly.

Mass is what allows the Sun to dominate all the planets, pulling them around. Technically, our Sun is a G-type main-sequence star, which means it fuses about 600 million tons of hydrogen with helium every second. It also converts about 4 million tons of matter into energy as a by-product. When the Sun dies, it will become a red giant, expanding and swallowing the Earth and everything on it. But don't worry: That won't happen for another 5 billion years.

  17. If two pieces of metal of the same element touch each other in space, they will self-align and stick together forever This amazing effect is called cold welding. It happens because the atoms at the edges of two pieces of pure (undoped) metal can no longer distinguish where they belong. Therefore, these atoms automatically bond with neighboring atoms, belonging to the neighboring piece of metal, causing them to stick together. The interesting thing is that cold welding never happens in Earth's atmosphere, because there are always water and air molecules separating the two pieces of metal, no matter how close you put them together. . The cold welding that occurs in the vacuum of space is an effect that has a lot of implications for the construction and repair of spacecraft and the future of construction in the vacuum.
