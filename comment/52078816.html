Socotra is a small archipelago consisting of 4 small islands located in the Indian Ocean, of which the largest island is Socotra, accounting for 95% of the archipelago, the remaining 3 small islands are called Abd al Kuri, Samhah and Darsa respectively. . Socotra Island has a special geographical position, part of the island of the Republic of Yemen. The island seems to be completely isolated from other lands, having undergone complex geological formations, now the whole island is a combination of ancient granite massifs, limestone cliffs and rocky plateaus. Very special ecosystem Socotra today is likened to a collection of the strangest and richest living creatures on the planet. This archipelago is long geologically isolated and the harsh climatic conditions of severe drought have created an extremely spectacular flora. According to a study by the Middle East Crop Research Center, up to 307,825 (or 37%) of the plant species here are endemic. Of which, there are 27 extremely rare species listed in the Red Book that are being protected.

Socotra Island also surprises people with more than 800 species of plants and animals that you will not find anywhere on this planet. One of the most distinctive plants of this archipelago is the dragon's blood tree. The sap of the dragon's blood tree was used by the ancients as a dye, and today people use it to paint and varnish furniture and wooden objects. There are also rare plants such as: endemic aloe vera, giant succulent plant Dorstenia gigas, rare pomegranate Punica protopunica, cucumber tree Dendrosicyos Socotranus...
