Bắt đầu

Morphology and taxonomy

(1)These fossils differ from those of chronologically later forms of early H. sapiens, (2)such as Cro-Magnon found in Europe and other parts of the world, (3)in that their morphology has many archaic features not typical of H. sapiens ((4)although modern human skulls do differ across the globe).

(5)Despite the archaic features, these specimens were argued to represent the direct ancestors of modern Homo sapiens sapiens which, (6)according to the "recent African origin (RAO)" or "out of Africa" model,(7) developed shortly after this period (Khoisan mitochondrial divergence dated not later than 110,000 BCE) in Eastern Africa.(8) "The many morphological features shared by the Herto crania and AMHS,(9) to the exclusion of penecontemporaneous Neanderthals, (10)provide additional fossil data excluding Neanderthals from a significant contribution to the ancestry of modern humans."

(11)A 2005 potassium-argon dating of volcanic tuff associated with the Omo remains showed them to date from about 195,000 years ago, (12)making them older than the idaltu fossils and the earliest known remains of anatomically modern humans.

(5) Mặc dù các tính năng cổ xưa, những mẫu được lập luận để đại diện cho tổ tiên trực tiếp của hiện đại Homo sapiens sapiens đó, (6) theo "nguồn gốc châu Phi gần đây (RAO)" hoặc "ra khỏi châu Phi" mô hình, (7) phát triển ngay sau khi giai đoạn này (Khoisan phân kỳ mitochondrial ngày không muộn hơn 110.000 TCN) ở Đông Phi. "
