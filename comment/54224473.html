  1. Cone snail

10 most dangerous animals in the world - 2Click to enlarge photo Found in hot water in the tropics (Caribbean, Hawaii, and Indonesia), you can easily recognize it with its yellow-brown outer shell, often living in shallow depths near shore, near coral reefs, rock formations, and under the sand. The poison prevents nerve cells from connecting with each other, leaving the victim instantly paralyzed, and only taking enough time to finish a cigarette before dying.

  1. Poison Dart . Golden Frog

  1. Box jellyfish

10 most dangerous animals in the world - 4Click to enlarge image The box jellyfish floats (or travels at nearly 8km per hour) in the Indo-Pacific waters north of Australia, a nearly invisible, transparent invertebrate classified by the Australian National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration. into one of the most venomous marine animals in the world. Their boxy bodies can contain up to 15 tentacles in the corners, each more than 20cm long, containing thousands of spiny cells whose toxins attack simultaneously the heart, nervous system and skin cells. The venom is so powerful and overwhelming that hundreds of victims each year, go into shock and die of heart failure before disembarking. Even with the luck of being taken to the hospital and given the antidote, the survivor remained in pain for several weeks afterward and carried nasty scars from the animal's tentacles.

Cá Nóc

Cá nóc thường có ở vùng biển nhiệt đới trên toàn cầu, đặc biệt là ở Nhật, Trung Quốc và Philippines. Mặc dù chúng là động vật có xương sống độc thứ hai trên hành tinh (sau ếch vàng phi tiêu), nhưng chúng có thể nguy hiểm hơn vì chất neurotoxin (hay còn gọi là tetrodotoxin) nằm ở nhiều nơi như trong da cá, mô cơ, gan, thận và tuyến sinh dục. Cá nóc được sử dụng làm món fugu ở Nhật, nhưng chỉ các đầu bếp được đào tạo bài bản mới được phép nấu món này. Chất độc tetrodotoxin độc gấp 1.200 lần so với xyanua, có thể gây chóng mặt, nôn mửa, loạn nhịp, khó thở, liệt cơ và, nếu không được điều trị, sẽ dẫn đến tử vong.

Puffer fish

There are frequent puffer fish in the tropics around the globe, especially in Japan, China and the Philippines. Although they are the second most poisonous vertebrate on the planet (after gold darts), they can be more dangerous because the nerve agent (also known as tetrodotoxin) is found in many places such as in fish skin, tissue muscles, liver, kidneys and gonads. Puffer fish is used in fugu in Japan, but only properly trained first cooks are allowed to cook it. The poison tetrodotoxin is 1,200 times more toxic than cyanide, and can cause madness, arrhythmias, shortness of breath, muscle paralysis, and, if corrected, death.

  1. Cedar flies

10 most dangerous animals in the world - 8Click to enlarge photo Considered the deadliest fly in the world, the tsetse fly is a small insect measuring between 8 and 17 mm, about the size of a housefly - commonly found in sub-Saharan countries, including Sudan, the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Angola. While they themselves are bloodsuckers, their real fear lies with the protozoan parasites they transmit, called Trypanosomes. These pathogens are responsible for African wet sleeping sickness, a disease marked by neurological and meningoencephalitic symptoms that include behavioral changes, poor coordination, as well as disturbances in the cycle sleep. It can be fatal if left untreated. Although there is no vaccine or preventive medicine, people can protect themselves by wearing neutral-colored clothing (tsetse flies are attracted to light and dark colors, especially blue). Stay out of the bushes during the day and use permethrin bug spray when traveling to more remote areas.

  1. Mosquitoes

10 most dangerous animals in the world - 9Click to enlarge image The smallest mosquito, only about 3mm in size, is classified as a dangerous animal because it causes a large number of deaths every year by spreading various pathogens. This nasty insect, mainly mosquitoes of the genera Aedes, Anopheles, and Culex - is the main cause of malaria, chikungunya, encephalitis, dorsal fin, yellow fever, dengue fever, western river virus Nile, and the Zika virus, infect about 700 million people and kill about 725,000 each year. According to the World Health Organization, more than half of the human population is currently at risk of mosquito-borne diseases because these animals are attracted to body heat and human CO2 emissions. The best prevention is to use an insect repellent with highly active ingredients such as DEET and picaridin.
