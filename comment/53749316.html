  1. Trải nghiệm tàu điện tự động dài nhất thế giới

Để giải quyết tình trạng giao thông lộn xộn, chính quyền Dubai đã bỏ ra khoảng 6 năm để lên kế hoạch, thiết kế và thi công hệ thống tàu điện vĩ đại mang tên Dubai Metro. Riêng nhà ga còn được xây dựng như một khách sạn hoành tráng để giúp khách có thể thoải mái nghỉ ngơi trong lúc chờ tàu.

  1. Experience the world's longest automatic tram

To solve the chaotic traffic situation, the Dubai government spent about 6 years planning, designing and constructing the great metro system called Dubai Metro. Nearly 40km long, this is considered the longest tram system in the world. More importantly, everything from the station to the trains is automated. The station itself was built as a monumental hotel to help guests relax while waiting for the train.

  1. “Bid” when buying gold at Gold Souk

Gold Souk gold market in Dubai is a destination chosen by many people, because of its splendid beauty, always sparkling with thousands of precious stones and precious metals displayed in small stalls. The famous market is located in an old street about 1km long, with about 500 stalls, each shop is only a few tens of square meters wide.

Gold is an item that is exempt from all taxes and fees in the United Arab Emirates, including Dubai, so gold at Gold Souk is ranked among the cheapest in the world. And instead of selling gold in ounces or grams like other countries, here gold is sold by "pounds". Gold price is calculated based on gold weight and age. On each shop window, the gold price in carats is updated daily by electronic boards, the conversion rate from US dollars to Dirhams (Dubai's currency) is also continuously published.

  1. One-of-a-kind Pets

In Dubai, pets are an integral part of people's daily lives. People can carry animals in the car, take them for a walk, watch the street together, even let the animals sit in their cars. Unlike Westerners who keep dogs and cats in their homes, Dubai's pets are tigers, lions, and cheetahs - animals that visitors are afraid of at first glance. Visitors to the streets of Dubai may be shocked when they see a picture of a tiger sitting on a supercar or a leopard playing with its owner to the fact that people ride on the back of a ferocious lion.
