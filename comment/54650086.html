THE DROP OF HONEY

A hunter and his hound, high in the desert mountains, found a cave. The hunter in the cave found a hollow, and the hollow was filled with the most beautiful, clear honey. He took his container, filled it with honey, and left the cave to come down from mountains. He was not in his own land.

And there in the town that he entered, he saw an oil seller. He went into that stall and offered his honey for sale - perhaps an exchange for some oil? And the oil seller was intrigued. As he was tasting the honey, one drop of honey fell upon the ground.

Some flies buzzed, swarming around honey. And as they swarmed there, some birds flew down to peck and eat the insects. But as the birds flew, darting among those insects, the cat that belonged to the oil seller leapt upon a bird and killed it. And as the cat leapt upon the bird, the hound of the hunter leapt forward and killed the cat.

The oil seller, full and fury, kicked the hound so hard that the hound was killed.

The hunter drew his hunting knife and killed the oil seller.

Those who were outside the stall came rushing in. They beat the foreigner to death.

Word reached the people - in the hunter's own land - of his murder. They sent people from his village over the bored and they killed many of the men in that town.

When the King of that land heard about this situation, he gathered a great army and waged war against his neighbour. And the kingdom retaliated.

Wars raged for many years. And wars have raged for many years since that time: two great enemies those neighbouring lands. And all because of one drop of money.

Always remember your actions have consequences.
