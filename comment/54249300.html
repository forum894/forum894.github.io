4. The magic mirror

A classic story, the King of Granada is looking for a wife, but there is one stipulation: whoever wants to be his wife must look into a magic mirror that judges goodness of her character, and will show as many blemishes on the mirror as she has made mistakes in her life. All of the women, at first eager to marry the king, refuse to look into the mirror. Only one comes forward: a lowly shepherdess. Upon being challenged by the magic mirror, the shepherdess says that she is not afraid because everyone makes mistakes and can be forgiven. After she looks into the mirror, the king reveals that there was no magic mirror, but that he was instead testing their confidence in themselves. The women are all dismayed when the King proposes to the shepherdess on the spot. They live happily ever after and the people never forget the lesson they learned that day.

Never be embarrassed of your mistakes. Embrace them and grow.

Stipulation (n.): điều kiện

Goodness (n.): lòng tốt

Blemish (n.): nhược điểm, thiếu sót

Eager (adj.): háo hức

Shepherdess (n.): cô gái chăn cừu

Reveal (v.): tiết lộ, nói thật

Dismayed (adj.): thất vọng

Propose (v.): cầu hôn

On the spot (adv.): ngay lập tức

Embrace (v.): giữ chặt, trân trọng
